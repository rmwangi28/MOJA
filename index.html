A Journey Through Culture, Wildlife and Adventure

People and Culture

Kenya has more than 40 ethnic groups, including the Maasai, Kikuyu, Luo, and Luhya. Each ethnic group has its unique language, customs, dances, art and contributing to Kenya's diverse cultural landscape.

Wildlife

Kenya, located in East Africa, is known for its breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and vibrant cultures. From the sweeping plains of the Maasai Mara to the stunning beaches of Mombasa, Kenya offers an experience like no other.

Climate

Kenya is known for its hot, dry climate, with wet seasons and dry winters. The average temperature is around 28°C (82°F).

Cuisine

Traditional Kenyan food is as mixed and diverse as its tribe's history and landscapes. Each tribal area has its own specialties.

Safari

Enjoy a vacation to Kenyan Safaris. Kenya has over 25 national parks, 18 national reserves and 6 marine parks/reserves.

Kenya's History

Kenya gained independence from British colonial rule on December 12, 1963, and Jomo Kenyatta became the first president.
