A Journey Through Culture, Wildlife and Adventure

People and Culture

Kenya has more than 40 ethnic groups, including the Maasai, Kikuyu, Luo, and Luhya. Each ethnic group has its unique language, customs, dances, art and contributing to Kenya's diverse cultural landscape.

Wildlife

Kenya, located in East Africa, is known for its breathtaking landscapes, diverse wildlife, and vibrant cultures. From the sweeping plains of the Maasai Mara to the stunning beaches of Mombasa, Kenya offers an experience like no other.

Climate

Kenya is known for its hot, dry climate, with wet seasons and dry winters. The average temperature is around 28°C (82°F).

Cuisine

Traditional Kenyan food is as mixed and diverse as its tribe's history and landscapes. Each tribal area has its own specialties.

Safari

Enjoy a vacation to Kenyan Safaris. Kenya has over 25 national parks, 18 national reserves and 6 marine parks/reserves.

Kenya's History

Kenya gained independence from British colonial rule on December 12, 1963, and Jomo Kenyatta became the first president.

Cuisine

Beef and Ugali

Beef and ugali is a beloved staple meal in Kenya, combining tender, saucy beef stew with ugali. The beef is typically simmered with onions, tomatoes, garlic, and spices until it's fall-apart soft.

Kenyan Chapati

Soft, layered flatbread, similar to Indian paratha, and is a popular staple in Kenya. It's made with all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, and oil, resulting in a flavorful and pliable bread. The dough is rolled, brushed with oil, folded, and then rolled out again, creating layers that become soft and flaky when cooked. Kenyan chapati is enjoyed at any time of the day, with stews, soups, or tea.

Chipsi Mayai

Swahili for chips and eggs, is a popular street food. In it's basic form, chips and eggs is a simple potato-egg omelette. It is served with french fries, kachumbari or pili-pili (spices)

Mahindi Choma

Grilled corn over an open flame, also a street food that is served with pili pili(spices)

Chai ya tangawizi

Sweet milk tea infuzed with ginger. Taken in the morning with breakfast served alongside chapati and mandazi. Known for its soothing qualities, it's particulary enjoyed on cold days.

Githeri

Githeri is a traditional kenyan dish consisting of maize(corn) and beans cooked together in a pot of water until tender. It is a staple food for communties in Central and Eastern Kenya, including the Kikuyu, Meru, Mbeere, Embu and Kamba people.

Kachumbari

Kachumbari is a fresh tomato, onion, chili pepper, cilantro and salt. In Kenya, it is used as a condiment served with pilau, mukimo or a meal with nyama choma(roasted meat) and ugali.

Kebab

Also known as minced kebabs, are a popular East African street food. They are made with minced beef or goat, mixed with spices, herbs, and onions. Kebabs are deep fried resulting in a crispy exterior and tender interior. They are often served with kachumbari or dipping sauces.

Kenyan Pancakes

In swahili known as vibibi, kenyan pancakes are a type of thin, crepe like pancake made with milk and sugar. They are a popular breakfast or snack in Kenya.

Kenyan Pilau

Kenyan Pilau is a flavorful rice dish, cooked for special occasions, used with aromatic spices and the choice of meat (usually beef, chicken, or goat) cooked in rich stock.

Kenyan Sausage

Smokies are a savory, midly spiced sausage made from finely ground beef, pork, or chicken. Smokies are beloved street food staple across Kenya, served hot from roadside vendors, often split and stuffed into a bun with a dollop of kachumbari. Kenyan sausages are a fusion of British colonial influence and local flavor, now completely integrated into the country’s urban food culture.

Maru Bhajia

Maru Bhajia is Kenya's beloved take on spiced potato fritters, with deep roots in Indian cuisine and a homegrown twist that’s become a street food classic. Thinly sliced potatoes are coated in a seasoned gram flour (besan) batter, infused with aromatic spices like cumin, coriander, chili, and fresh chopped dhania (cilantro). Maru Bhajia apart is the delicate balance of flavor: a whisper of heat, earthy undertones, and the fresh zing of herbs.

Mabuyu (Candied Baobab Seeds)

Mabuyu is a bold, tangy, and vibrantly colored Kenyan street snack made from baobab seeds soaked and coated in a spiced, sweet-sour candy glaze.Traditionally sold in small paper cones or plastic jars at school gates, market stalls, and matatu stops, mabuyu is a nostalgic treat for many who grew up in coastal or urban Kenya.

Mandazi

East African Beignets. Mandazi is a sweet, airy fried dough native to East Africa, especially in Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. Mandazi are lightly sweetened, pillowy triangles or rounds with hints of coconut milk, cardamom, and sometimes a touch of nutmeg. Mandazi isn’t just food—it’s comfort, community, and the aroma of home kitchens filling with laughter and oil-sweet steam.

Matoke

Matoke is a traditional East African dish made from starchy green bananas, gently simmered down into a rich, savory stew. Native to Uganda but beloved across Kenya, Tanzania, and Rwanda, matoke starts with peeled plantain-like bananas that are steamed or boiled until tender, then mashed or cooked whole in a tomato-onion gravy bursting with garlic, oil, and spices.

Mukimo

Mukimo is mashed green maize and potato dish. Mukimo is a traditional Kenyan mash from the Kikuyu community, made by combining soft-boiled potatoes with green peas, maize (corn), and pumpkin or stinging nettle leaves (managu or kahurura)—mashed together into a vibrant green, earthy dish that’s as nourishing as it is comforting.

Mutura

Kenyan blood sausage, mutura is Kenya’s iconic blood sausage—bold, spiced, and straight from the grill. Made with minced meat, onions, fresh herbs and spices. Shared between friends mutura is the street food with soul.

Ndengu Stew

A hearty stew made from mung beans simmered in a rich, savory blend of onions, tomatoes, garlic and aromatic spices. Loved for its high protein content and affordability, ndengu stew is a go to for many kenyan households.

Nyama Choma

Nyama Choma means "roast meat" in Swahili, is Kenya's ultimate communal dish. Grilled meat done slow and smoky. Made from goat, the meat is salted, sometimes marinated then fire roasted over open flames until the outside is crisp and charred while the inside is juicy and tender.

Omena

Omena is a traditional Kenyan dish made from tiny silver fish. Lake Victoria’s own treasure, sun-dried and then fried or stewed into a rich, umami-packed delicacy. Omena is often sautéed with garlic and tomatoes until they form a thick, aromatic stew, or simply fried until crunchy and golden. Served with ugali and leafy greens like sukuma wiki, omena is humble, hearty, and deeply satisfying.

Smocha

Chapati wrap with smokie, Smocha is a kenyan street food combo where a juicy smokie wrapped in a soft, flaky chapati. It's a grab and go snack. Hot off the pan, the chapati is rolled around the smokie like a Kenyan burrito, often slathered in ketchup, chili sauce, or a fresh dollop of kachumbari if the vendor’s feeling generous. It’s got that salty, savory sausage bite cushioned by warm, buttery layers of chapati.

Sukuma Wiki

Sukuma wiki is a kenyan collard green. A hearty, affordable leafy green that stretches meals and fills bellies across Kenya. These tender, collard greens are sautéed with onions, tomatoes, and garlic, often with a splash of oil and a pinch of salt, until tender. Sometimes paired with ugali, beef stew or rice.

Soup Pilaf

Soup Pilaf is kenyan beef stew with carrots and potatoes. Tender chunks of beef simmered low and slow with carrots, potatoes, and a savory broth seasoned with garlic, onions, and warming spices. The “soup” in the name refers to the stew’s rich, flavorful gravy that’s perfect for soaking up with pilau rice, chapati, or ugali.

Ugali

Kenyan cornmeal staple, Ugali is a simple, sturdy cornmeal staple. Made by stirring maize flour into boiling water until thick. It is the backbone of countless meals, a hearty scoop alongside nyama choma, sukuma wiki or stew. Designed to be rolled into a ball with your fingers, Its texture is firm yet tender, with just enough heft to hold its shape and soak up sauces without falling apart.

Matumbo

Also known as tripe, matumbo is Kenya's delicacy. Beef stomach slowly cooked to tender perfection in a savory stew brimming with onions, tomatoes, garlic and traditional spices. It's a chewy, hearty and flavorful dish. Often enjoyed as part of a communal meal with ugali and sukuma wiki.

Tusker Beer

Kenya's classic lager. Named after the legendary elephant "Tusker" this beer carries a bold heritage dating back to 1922, making it one of East Africa's oldest and most beloved brews. Tusker is a light beer enjoyed after a long day's work.

Irio

In the kikuyu tribe "irio" means food. It is a hearty and nutritious side dish, it is made with mashed potatoes, maize(corn), peas, and sometimes spinach. During the dry season, dried beans and maize were added to the mashed potatoes.

Samosas

Samosas are golden, triangle-shaped pockets of joy. Crisp on the outside, bursting with spicy, savory filling on the inside. A beloved snack across Kenya with Indian roots. Introduced by Indian immigrants during the early 20th century, samosas quickly became integrated into Kenyan food culture, evolving from a diaspora delicacy into a mainstream must-have.

Mshikaki

Mshikaki is a Swahili-style meat skewer, marinated cubes of beef, goat, or chicken threaded onto sticks and slow-grilled over open charcoal flames until smoky, juicy, and just a little charred on the edges. Mshikaki reflects the cultural fusion of the Swahili Coast. A delicious product of African, Arab, and South Asian culinary influences.

Split Eggs

Mayai Pasua in Swahili is a popular Kenyan street snack. Hard-boiled eggs sliced in half and generously filled with kachumbari. Sometimes dusted with a pinch of salt of chili powder think of this snack as Kenya's deviled egg.

Goat Head Soup

Supu ya kichwa ya mbuzi is a deep nourishing broth made by slow cooking a whole goat's head, tongue, cheeks, brain and all. Infused with garlic, ginger, onions, and sometimes chili pepper. Goat holds a place of honor. The head being a prized and symbolic part of the animal, is often reserved for the most respected individuals.

Chevda

Chevdo or Kenyan mix is a crispy, spicy, and addictive snack mix that reflects the fusion of Kenyan and Indian culinary traditions. Brought over by the Indian diaspora during colonial times, Chevda has found a second home in Kenya. It’s a common snack during tea time.
